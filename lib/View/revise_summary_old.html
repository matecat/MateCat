How MateCat Helps Objective Quality Measurements

MateCat uses an analytical approach to assess quality. It sets a maximum number of tolerated issues (1 Enhancement = ${php: constant( get_class( reviseClass ) . "::SERV_VALUE_MINOR" )} Error) and uses the data provided by the reviser to calculate an objective score.

The level of quality can be agreed upon with your provider. For some projects "Good" or "Acceptable" may be ok, whereas others will require an "Excellent" score.

Score Calculation

MateCat uses the following formula to calculate the number of tolerated issues for the Job:

N° of Tolerated Issues per Job = ( N° of Payable Words / ${php: number_format( constant( get_class( reviseClass ) . "::WORD_INTERVAL" ) )} ) * N° of Tolerated Issues for ${php: number_format( constant( get_class( reviseClass ) . "::WORD_INTERVAL" ) )} words

For each type of issue, MateCat calculates the score this way:

N° of Issues Found / N° of Tolerated Issues per Job

The Final Score is the average of the single category scores.
